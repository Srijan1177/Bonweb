London, United Kingdom

The Great City - Where history meets modernity in perfect harmony

Big Ben & Houses of Parliament

Iconic symbol of London featuring the famous clock tower and the seat of British democracy, located along the River Thames.

Buckingham Palace

Official residence of the British monarch, featuring the famous Changing of the Guard ceremony and stunning royal architecture.

British Museum

World-renowned museum housing over 8 million works, including the Rosetta Stone and Parthenon sculptures.

Borough Market

London's most famous food market, offering fresh produce, artisanal foods, and international cuisine in a historic setting.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to visit London?
Spring (March to May) and early autumn (September to October) offer pleasant weather and fewer crowds. Summer is popular but busy, while winter is cold but magical with Christmas decorations.
How do I get around London?
London has an excellent public transport system including the Underground (Tube), buses, and trains. Get an Oyster card or use contactless payment for the best rates. Walking is also great for exploring central areas.
Is London expensive to visit?
London can be expensive, but there are many free attractions like museums, parks, and walking tours. Plan ahead, use public transport, and consider staying in less central areas to save money.
